06 — Brand Voice

Personality.

Rally speaks like the smartest person at the after-party — informed, effortless, never trying too hard.

We are
  • Confident. We know it works. We don't over-explain it.
  • Minimal. Short sentences. White space. Let the product speak.
  • Insider. We speak to people who live in this world — not at them.
  • Premium. Every word earns its place.
  • Dry-witty. A raised eyebrow, not a punchline.
We are not
  • Clinical. No "clinically proven" language unless for technical pages.
  • Bro-y. Not "crush your hangover." Not "beast mode recovery."
  • Preachy. We don't lecture about drinking. We solve a real need.
  • Discount. No "deal," "cheap," "budget" vocabulary anywhere.
  • Hype-y. No exclamation marks unless truly exceptional.
Write like this
  • "Before you drink. After a night well spent."
  • "DHM helps your body process alcohol while you rest."
  • "One jelly. That's it."
  • "We put 20 ingredients in it so you don't have to think about it."
  • "Rally. Take it tonight."
Not like this
  • "DESTROY your hangover with our POWERFUL formula!"
  • "Get WASTED and wake up FINE!!!"
  • "Our proprietary blend is backed by extensive clinical research studies."
  • "The BEST hangover cure on the market, guaranteed."
  • "Party hard, recover harder."

08 — Design Principles

How we look.

Four principles that govern every creative decision.

01

Restraint over decoration

Remove before you add. Every element must justify its presence. White space is not empty — it's confident.

02

Warmth over sterility

Cream over white. Bone over slate. Gold over yellow. Plum over black. Everything has warmth — we're premium, not cold.

03

Editorial not clinical

We communicate ingredients and science through the aesthetic lens of a luxury magazine, not a pharmacy shelf.

04

Night to morning arc

Our visual world spans from deep navy (nightlife) to warm cream (morning light). Both are Rally. Both are ours.

11 — Photography

Visual direction.

Three photographic worlds. All of them are Rally.

Night world

The Night

Dimly lit bars and clubs. Warm candlelight. Champagne reflections. Crowded rooftops. Deep shadows with amber highlights. Shot on film aesthetic. Never chaotic — curated, editorial nightlife.

Morning world

The Morning

Sun-drenched apartments. Linen sheets. Coffee and Rally on a marble countertop. Effortless morning energy. Clean light, warm tones, soft focus. The reward for taking Rally before the night out.

Product world

The Product

Clean flat-lays on linen or bone surfaces. Packet next to botanicals — turmeric root, ginger, dried jujube. Macro shots of ingredients. Editorial, not clinical. Think Aesop or Glossier, not CVS.

Shoot this

Low-light, warm-toned bar interiors — candles, amber glows, bokeh
Morning light on skin, sheets, and surfaces. Effortless, not posed
Product on linen/bone with natural botanicals and textures
Real people. Diverse. Confident. Well-dressed but not costumey
Film grain, soft tones, slightly warm color grading

Never this

Sloppy party scenes — red cups, vomiting references, recklessness
Overly bright studio-white clinical photography
Stock photo energy — forced smiles, generic concepts
Cluttered backgrounds, multiple competing focal points
Heavy filters, oversaturated colors, neon aesthetics

12 — Usage Rules

The guardrails.

Consistency protects the brand. These are not suggestions.

Always

Set the wordmark in Cormorant Garamond Light with wide tracking (0.25–0.30em)
Use warm linen (#F5EDE0) or bone (#E0D4BC) for light backgrounds — never pure white (#ffffff)
Use the deep navy-to-white contrast when possible — it's the brand's core visual tension
Maintain generous whitespace — aim for at least 40% breathing room on any composition
Keep warm gold (#C4954A) as an accent, not a dominant background — use linen and bone for surfaces
Use DM Mono for all labels, tags, and metadata — never decorative
Write in short, declarative sentences. Maximum 2 sentences for any body copy block.

Never

Stretch, skew, or outline the wordmark
Place the logo on a busy photographic background without a color block
Use green — not in the palette, not as an accent, not for "organic" or "wellness" signaling
Use more than 3 type sizes on a single layout
Add drop shadows, gradients, or textures to the wordmark
Use exclamation marks in headlines or product names
Mix DM Sans and DM Mono in a single text block — they serve different roles
